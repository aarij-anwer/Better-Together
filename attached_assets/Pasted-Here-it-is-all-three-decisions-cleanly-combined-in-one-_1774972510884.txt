## Product Decisions & UX Guidelines

### 1. Dashboard Summary (Home Page Behavior)

The home page should include a **lightweight summary of the user’s progress for today**, while remaining minimal and action-oriented.

#### Recommendation

Include a small **“Today” summary strip at the top of the dashboard**.

#### Example

* “Today: 30 pushups • 5,000 steps”
  or
* “Today’s Progress: 2 of 3 challenges completed”

---

#### Key Principles

* This is **not a full analytics view**
* Do **not** include charts, graphs, or historical breakdowns
* Keep it **simple, glanceable, and action-oriented**
* The goal is to:

  * Reinforce daily progress
  * Remind users to complete remaining challenges
  * Direct attention back to active challenges

---

#### Why This Matters

* Helps establish a **daily habit loop**
* Gives users a quick sense of progress across multiple challenges
* Prevents the dashboard from feeling like a passive list
* Adds subtle urgency without overwhelming the user

---

#### Important Constraint

Keep this section **visually lightweight and secondary** to the challenge list. The primary goal of the home page is still:

> Quickly getting users back into their active challenges to log progress

### 2. Challenge Start Date

For the MVP, challenges should **default to starting immediately (today)**, while still allowing users to optionally select a future start date.

#### Recommendation

* Default the start date to **today**
* Allow users to optionally choose a **future start date**
* Keep the input simple (e.g. date picker)

---

#### Key Principles

* Preserve speed and low friction in the creation flow
* Support simple scheduling use cases (e.g. “starting Monday”)
* Avoid overcomplicating the experience

---

#### Why This Matters

* Maintains fast creation for most users (no extra input required)
* Supports real-world scenarios:

  * Friends planning ahead
  * Group challenges starting at the same time
* Keeps flexibility without sacrificing simplicity

---

#### UX Behavior

* If no date is selected → challenge starts **immediately (today)**
* If a future date is selected:

  * Challenge is created in a **“not started” state**
  * Users can still join via invite link
  * Logging is **disabled until the start date**

---

#### Important Constraints

* Do not introduce complex scheduling logic
* Do not allow editing the start date after creation (for MVP)
* Keep UI simple and unobtrusive (date picker should not dominate the form)

### 3. Overall App Feel & Design Direction

The app should feel **clean and minimal, with subtle energy**, rather than loud or overly gamified.

#### Recommendation

Adopt a **clean, modern design with focused visual hierarchy**, using energy sparingly to reinforce progress and action.

---

#### Design Direction

* Clean layout with strong spacing and typography
* Minimal clutter — only essential elements on screen
* Clear visual hierarchy (today’s progress is dominant)
* Subtle motion and feedback (e.g. progress ring animation on log)
* Limited color palette with one primary accent color

---

#### Tone

* Calm and focused, not chaotic
* Motivating without being overwhelming
* Social and competitive, but not aggressive

---

#### Inspirations

* Notion (clean structure)
* Apple Fitness (focused progress visuals)
* Minimal fitness trackers (clarity over noise)

---

#### Why This Matters

* Keeps the experience approachable and easy to use daily
* Reduces cognitive load during the core loop (logging progress)
* Makes the product feel more “premium” and intentional
* Supports long-term retention by avoiding fatigue

---

#### Important Constraint

Avoid:

* Overly gamified UI (badges everywhere, excessive animations)
* Loud or cluttered layouts
* Too many competing elements on one screen

The design should always reinforce the core loop:

> Open app → log progress → see progress → stay motivated
